Cookie Policy

Last updated:

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device when you visit our website. They help us provide you with a better browsing experience by remembering your preferences and allowing us to understand how you use our site.

info Quick Fact: Cookies cannot harm your computer or mobile device. They are simply text files that contain small amounts of information.

2. How We Use Cookies

Aluresoft uses cookies for various purposes to enhance your experience on our website:

settingsEssential Functions
  • Remember your preferences
  • Keep you logged in
  • Ensure website security
  • Enable core functionality
analyticsAnalytics & Performance
  • Understand website usage
  • Improve website performance
  • Track popular content
  • Identify technical issues

3. Types of Cookies We Use

3.1 Essential Cookies

Session Cookies: Temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser. These are essential for website functionality.
Security Cookies: Help protect against cross-site request forgery and other security threats.
Authentication Cookies: Remember that you're logged in and maintain your session.
warning Important: Essential cookies cannot be disabled as they are necessary for the website to function properly.

3.2 Analytics Cookies

Google Analytics: Helps us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting anonymous information.
Performance Monitoring: Tracks website speed and performance to help us improve user experience.
Usage Statistics: Provides insights into which pages are most popular and how users navigate our site.

3.3 Preference Cookies

Language Settings: Remember your preferred language and regional settings.
Display Preferences: Store your choices about layout, color scheme, or other customizable features.
Form Data: Remember information you've entered in forms to improve user experience.

4. Third-Party Cookies

We may use third-party services that set their own cookies. These services include:

Google Analytics

5. Cookie Duration

Cookies have different lifespans depending on their purpose:

Session Cookies: Deleted when you close your browser
Persistent Cookies: Remain on your device for a specified period (from days to years)
Secure Cookies: Only transmitted over encrypted connections (HTTPS)
HttpOnly Cookies: Not accessible through JavaScript, enhancing security
Cookie Retention Schedule
Cookie Type Duration Purpose
Session Until browser closes Essential functionality
Authentication 30 days Keep you logged in
Preferences 1 year Remember your settings
Analytics 2 years Website improvement

6. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

6.1 Browser Settings

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Here's how to manage cookies in popular browsers:

Chrome: Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies and other site data
Firefox: Settings > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Manage Website Data
Edge: Settings > Cookies and site permissions > Cookies and site data

6.2 Cookie Consent Management

Your Choices

When you first visit our website, you'll see a cookie consent banner allowing you to:

  • Accept all cookies
  • Reject non-essential cookies
  • Customize your cookie preferences
  • Learn more about our cookie usage

6.3 Opt-Out Links

You can opt out of specific tracking services:

  • Google Analytics Opt-out
  • Network Advertising Initiative Opt-out
  • Digital Advertising Alliance Opt-out

7. Impact of Disabling Cookies

warning Important Notice

Disabling certain cookies may impact your experience on our website:

Essential Cookies: Disabling these may prevent core website functionality from working properly
Preference Cookies: You may need to re-enter your preferences each time you visit
Analytics Cookies: We won't be able to improve our website based on usage data
All Cookies: Some features may not work correctly or at all
